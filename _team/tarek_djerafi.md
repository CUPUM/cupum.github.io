Tarek Djerafi received the B.Sc. degree from the Institut d'Aéronautique de Blida, Blida, Algeria, in 1998, and the M.A.Sc. and Ph.D. degrees (Hons.) in electrical engineering from the École Polytechnique de Montréal, Montréal, QC, Canada, in 2005 and 2011, respectively. He was with SCP SCIENCE, Montréal, as an EMC Expert from 2010 to 2011, a PostDoctoral Fellow with INRS-EMT, Montréal, from 2012 to 2014, and with École Polytechnique de Montréal from 2014 to 2015. He was a Research Associate with the Poly-Grames Research Center, Montréal, from 2015 to 2016. He is currently an Assistant Professor with the Institut National de Recherche Scientifique, Énergie Matériaux et Télécommunications, Montréal. His current research interests include telecommunication antennas, beam forming networks, and RF/millimeter-wave/terahertz components and systems design.
